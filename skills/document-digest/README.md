# Document Digest

A skill that summarizes a dense administrative, legal, insurance, trust, entity, subscription, or operating document into clear, plain-English notes — and surfaces the questions to put to counsel or an advisor. It does not provide legal, tax, or accounting advice.

## What it does

Produces a digest: document type, plain-English summary, key parties, key dates, obligations, risks or unusual terms, questions for counsel or advisor, and action items.

## Who uses it

Operators, chiefs of staff, and executive assistants who need to understand a document quickly before it goes to a professional.

## How to use it

1. Paste the document or a representative excerpt (see [SKILL.md](SKILL.md) → *Inputs*). Provide only sanitized material, and use an approved environment for sensitive documents.
2. Invoke the skill (see the *Example request* in [SKILL.md](SKILL.md)).
3. Treat the digest as a **plain-English read, not a legal interpretation.** Verify dates and obligations against the document.
4. Route the *Questions for counsel or advisor* to the appropriate professional before acting.

## What it will not do

- It will not provide legal, tax, or accounting advice or interpret the document as a professional.
- It will not invent clauses, dates, or assume "standard" terms.
- It will not recommend signing, declining, or executing the document.
